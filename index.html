What is TAB?

Testing, Adjusting and Balancing

TAB is the systematic process of verifying that all mechanical systems in a building, including HVAC, air distribution, hydronic systems, and controls, perform in accordance with the design intent and the owner's requirements.

Without TAB, a building may be commissioned with systems that are out of balance, consuming excess energy, failing to maintain comfort conditions, or operating in ways that will cause premature equipment failure. TAB identifies these deficiencies before they become expensive problems.

The TAB Standard
Balanced as Designed follows ASHRAE, SMACNA, and AABC standards for all testing and balancing work, ensuring your documentation is accepted by engineers, owners, and regulatory authorities.
The TAB Process
1
Pre-TAB Review
Review design documents, equipment schedules, and system schematics to establish baseline expectations.
2
System Testing
Measure actual airflow, water flow, temperatures, pressures, and equipment performance against design values.
3
Adjusting
Adjust dampers, valves, fan speeds, and controls to bring systems into compliance with design specifications.
4
Balancing
Distribute airflow and water flow proportionally across all zones to achieve uniform comfort and efficiency.
5
TAB Report
Deliver a comprehensive report documenting all measurements, adjustments, and final verified performance data.

Why It Matters

What TAB Protects

01
Owner Investment

TAB verifies that the mechanical systems you paid for are actually performing as specified. Without it, you may accept a building with systems that will never meet design intent.

02
Occupant Comfort

Unbalanced systems create hot and cold spots, humidity problems, and air quality issues. TAB ensures every zone receives the airflow it needs for consistent comfort.

03
Energy Efficiency

Unbalanced systems consume significantly more energy than designed. TAB establishes the performance baseline that makes energy management possible.

04
Equipment Longevity

Systems operating outside design parameters wear out faster. TAB identifies and corrects conditions that cause premature equipment failure and costly replacements.
